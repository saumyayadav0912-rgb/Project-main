Traditional Food

Rajasthani food is known for its rich flavors and use of spices. The most famous dish is **Dal Baati Churma**, along with **Gatte ki Sabzi, Ker Sangri**, and **Laal Maas**. Sweets like **Ghevar** and **Mawa Kachori** are also widely loved.

Traditional Dress

Rajasthanâ€™s traditional dress is colorful and vibrant. Men wear **Pagri (turbans)**, **Angarkha**, and **Dhoti**, while women wear **Ghagra-Choli** with a beautifully embroidered **Odhni (veil)**. The bright colors reflect the lively spirit of the desert.

Festivals

Rajasthan celebrates many colorful festivals such as the **Pushkar Camel Fair**, **Teej**, **Gangaur**, and **Desert Festival** of Jaisalmer. These festivals include music, dance, puppet shows, camel races, and traditional fairs.

Music & Dance

Folk music and dance are at the heart of Rajasthani culture. The **Kalbelia dance**, performed by snake charmers, and **Ghoomar dance**, known for its graceful twirls, are world famous. Traditional instruments like the **Sarangi** and **Dholak** add charm to the performances.

Handicrafts

Rajasthan is famous for its **handicrafts, block printing, blue pottery, and jewelry**. The markets of Jaipur, Jodhpur, and Udaipur are filled with handmade items like puppets, carpets, and textiles. These crafts reflect the artistic traditions of Rajasthan.
